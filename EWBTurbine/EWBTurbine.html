Engineers Without Borders

Design of Teacher's Guide for Mini Wind Turbine Kits

Duration- June 2021, 3 weeks
Type- Solo project
Context- University Club Project
Skills- Graphic Design (for digital publication)

Project Context

The overall project is to encourage students to explore engineering and sustainability as a possible university major and eventual career by giving them materials to build a working mini-wind turbine that generates electricity.

This project was from Engineers Without Borders, a student-led club at the University of Washington. The club partnered with Renton School District and secured funding from a university group called the Campus Sustainability Fund.

On the left is an image of the completed kit (the box contains 10 mini-wind turbines kits).

My Role

I was the co-lead for this project and one of my roles was to design a manual guiding instructors on how to use the club-designed kits. I wrote most of the content with the rest written by other club members. For the design, I worked solo with some feedback from the other project lead.

Process

I prepared the informational content, set the style direction, then designed the manual by drafting with wireframes and creating the final look with Figma.

Informational Content

I began by listing out what informational content to include, based on our partner’s needs and requests. One important need was our partner school’s lecture-free teaching style and so I wrote educational prompts for instructors to ask and discuss with students. We focused on using brevity and simple language.

Style Direction

After setting up the content, I set a direction for the style. As a manual-type design, I decided to (visually) reference instruction manuals by creating vector-based images, and to make it clean and simple with a sans serif font and 2-color palette.

Content Organization for Usability

Then, I roughly organized the content on paper, keeping usability in mind (for instance, the assembly instructions and educational prompts are on the same page, so instructors can teach a class with just 1 page of the guide rather than flipping back and forth). I also focused on keeping the page count low so instructors could concentrate of teaching rather than flipping through the manual.

Design

I started the design with sketched out wireframes, then moved on to Figma for the final look. I went between sketching and Figma a couple times, reorganizing content for a compact use of space.

Final Design

Here is the final design. The school will start using the kits and this manual near the tail end of the 2021-22 school year.

Reflection

It's so important to be clear when communicating about project goals!

At first, we planned for the educational component on majors and sustainability to be conveyed through a powerpoint presentation. When our partner first mentioned that their classes would rarely have lectures, and would only short ones when they did, we thought they were asked for a short & condensed powerpoint. However, they actually wanted no presentation at all and we pivoted to educational prompts instead where we wrote discussion questions & answers for the instructors.
